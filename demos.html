COEQWAL visualization demos

Interactive prototypes you can try in the browser. Synthetic rehearsal data throughout. Back to working pages.

Interactive visualization prototypes, published here so you can try them directly and reply with reactions. Every prototype runs on synthetic rehearsal data — placeholder values with the right shape, not COEQWAL results (a badge on each page says so).

Orientation: how the library is organized

A reading frame for newcomers to the result library: start from where things stand today, then see how to read any result — a baseline, a climate future, and a chosen strategy laid out as four pathways. Sets up the way the other prototypes present results.

 Guided stepper

The chosen direction for the public website: a three-step walk through one strategy — where things stand today, what climate alone does, then what the strategy adds — in continuous tier space. Click through the steps; feedback on pacing and wording is especially useful.

 Stepper, location-spread variant

The same three-step idea told with one dot per location in a single animated column — vertical motion only, with trails marking the biggest movers. Explores whether the location-level spread reads better than sector means.

 Scenario columns by climate

Four climate columns, all 24 strategies slotted in each. Hover a dot to split it into baseline + climate + operations; click to pin. This figure also appears as Figure 2 in the location-brief prototype.

 Tier grid ("See who benefits")

Standalone version of the website's equity waffle — triangles and squares by tier and result category, with compare-to-baseline and color-mode toggles.

 Data in-depth explorer

Prototype for the second result pathway: raw model output rather than tiers. Pick a sector, variable, and location, then use the "Compare by" switch to overlay scenarios, climate futures, or locations — one dimension at a time. Plain-language summaries sit above each chart, with technical detail one click away; plotted data exports as CSV, charts as SVG.
Prototypes are working material; the public website versions may differ. Questions: Eric Danner.
