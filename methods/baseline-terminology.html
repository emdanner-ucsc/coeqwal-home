COEQWAL — The Landscape

2026-04-04

Status note (2026-06-09): the served hydroclimate set is BL + HC1 + HC2 only (per coeqwal_conventions.md, corrected 2026-06-09). There is no served HC3 — TaiESM1 (the model previously carrying the editorial "HC3" label) and EC-Earth3-Veg are run but not yet served. Where this doc enumerates "HC1, HC2, HC3", read "every served non-historical hydroclimate"; the concepts apply unchanged as new climates come online.

The problem

The word "baseline" currently refers to two conceptually distinct things in the project: the lived status quo (s0020 × Historical HC), and the counterfactual reference points produced by running current operations through alternative climate futures (s0020 × HC1, HC2, HC3). Calling both "baselines" collapses a distinction that is load-bearing for interpretation: one is empirical and grounded in current experience; the others are forward-looking projections of what would happen if climate changed but operations didn't. When the modifier gets dropped in conversation, workshop notes, or memory — and it will — the grounding is lost.

Terminology

The baseline

Definition: s0020 (current operations) × Historical hydroclimate. The one scenario-hydroclimate combination that represents the actual, experienced status quo.

Visual encoding: The single dot per sector or LOI sitting on the diagonal in the Historical HC view. This is the fixed origin for all geometric decompositions.

This is the only entity called "baseline" without qualification. When a visitor, workshop participant, or document says "the baseline," it means this and only this.

Climate-projected baselines

Definition: s0020 (current operations) × any non-historical hydroclimate (HC1, HC2, HC3). Full CalSim simulation runs that project current operations forward into alternative climate futures. These are not corrections or adjustments applied to the primary baseline — they are independent model runs with different climate inputs. They exist to isolate what climate contributes from what operational strategy contributes.

Visual encoding: Hollow circles on the diagonal in the All HC view, colored by hydroclimate. Each sits at (B(s, h), B(s, h)) for its respective HC.

Why "projected": The HC-paired references are forward-looking simulations — projections of where current operations would land under climates that haven't arrived yet. "Projected" conveys that a model was run, not that a number was corrected. It avoids the connotation of "adjusted" (which implies a mathematical transformation of existing data) and "reference" (which understates the structural role these points play as the denominators of the Δop calculation).

Why "baseline" is retained: These points represent unchanged operations. Dropping "baseline" from the label risks implying that something about the strategy has changed. Retaining it communicates that these are still current operations — the same policy, the same rules — just operating in a different climate. The modifier "climate-projected" carries the distinction from the primary baseline; the shared word "baseline" carries the shared structural role (the thing operational impact is measured from).

Scenario outcomes

Definition: Any alternative scenario a ≠ a₀ paired with any hydroclimate. The thing being evaluated. Always measured against its own HC-matched baseline (primary or climate-projected).

Visual encoding: Filled, colored dots displaced vertically from the diagonal. Their vertical distance from the diagonal equals Δop — the net operational impact.

The three-term vocabulary

The complete naming system:

TermWhat it isOperationsClimateRole in decomposition
Baselines0020 × HistoricalCurrentHistoricalFixed visual origin on diagonal; departure point for all comparisons
Climate-projected baselines0020 × HC1/HC2/HC3CurrentProjected futureHC-matched reference for computing Δop; encodes ΔHC relative to primary baseline
Scenario outcomeAlternative × any HCAlternativeAnyThe evaluated outcome; Δop = scenario outcome − matched baseline

Every scenario outcome is compared to a baseline. For Historical HC, that's the baseline. For any other HC, it's the climate-projected baseline for that HC. The word "baseline" appears in both because both play the same structural role — the denominator in the Δop calculation. The modifier tells you which climate context the reference lives in.

Usage in the decomposition

The terminology maps directly onto the decomposition algebra:

ΔHC = climate-projected baseline − baseline "How much does climate alone move the starting point from where we are today?"

Δop = scenario outcome − its matched baseline (primary or climate-projected) "What does the strategy contribute, measured against the appropriate starting point for that climate?"

The phrase "measured against the appropriate starting point for that climate" is the plain-language version of "evaluated against B(s, h)" — and the terminology makes it parseable without the algebra.

Application to current tool text

Tour step 5 (Climate futures)

Current: "...six hollow circles on the diagonal showing where each sector starts before any strategy is applied. The hollow circles tell the climate story..."

Revised: "...hollow circles on the diagonal showing where current operations would land under each climate future. These are the projected starting points — same operations, different climates. The spread of hollow circles along the diagonal tells the climate story..."

The revision names what the hollow circles are (projected starting points for current operations under different climates) rather than only what they show (the climate story). A visitor who reads "where each sector starts" may not understand that these represent full model simulations of current operations under projected climates.

Explore question eq-hc2 narrative

Current: "The hollow baseline circles on the diagonal show where each sector's starting point lands under each climate — that is the climate signal, independent of any strategy. The vertical gap between each filled dot and the diagonal shows what the strategy adds on top of that climate baseline..."

Revised: "The hollow circles on the diagonal show where current operations would land under each climate future — the climate-projected starting points. Their spread is the climate signal, independent of any strategy. The vertical gap between each filled dot and the diagonal shows what the strategy adds on top of the projected starting point for that climate..."

Tooltip for hollow circles (All HC view)

Current: (varies / not standardized)

Proposed: "Current operations under [Moderate-high climate stress]: Tier [value]"

This is short enough for a tooltip, identifies the operations (current), identifies the climate (by name), and gives the outcome. It does not use the word "baseline" in the tooltip itself — the concept is communicated by "current operations" — which avoids the modifier-dropping problem in the most space-constrained context.

Equity baselines / ghost circles narrative (formerly eq-eq3, now consolidated into eq-eq1/eq-eq2)

Current: "...the hollow circles show where each node sits under current operations; the filled dots show where they land under this alternative strategy."

This phrasing is already correct and does not need revision. The ghost circles in equity spread mode represent current operations under the active HC (typically Historical), and the text says exactly that. The term "baseline ghosts" in the toggle label could be revised to "current operations ghosts" or simply left as is — the toggle is a compact UI element where "baseline" is unambiguous because only one HC is active.

Edge cases

Single-HC views (Historical): In any view with only Historical HC selected, there is only one baseline and no climate-projected baselines exist. The term "baseline" is unambiguous. No modifier needed.

All-HC views with sector hulls: The hull encloses dots anchored to different climate-projected baselines. The hull's horizontal extent is the spread of climate-projected baselines along the diagonal. Descriptive phrasing: "the range of projected starting points across climate futures."

Centroid displacement from primary baseline: The staircase decomposition (section 3.2 of point_placement_geometry.md) moves from the baseline to the centroid in two steps: first along the diagonal (mean ΔHC — the average shift of the climate-projected baselines from the primary baseline), then vertically off the diagonal (mean Δop — the average operational impact). Descriptive phrasing: "On average across climate futures, the projected starting point shifts [direction] from today's baseline, and the strategy [improves/degrades] outcomes by [magnitude] on top of that shift."

Methods layer: The algebraic notation B(s, h) covers both the primary baseline (h = BL) and climate-projected baselines (h ≠ BL) under a single symbol. This is correct and should not change — the algebra doesn't need the distinction because h already parameterizes it. The terminology distinction is for human communication, not for the math.
