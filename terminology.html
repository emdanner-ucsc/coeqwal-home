Terminology

Dispreferred term: "outcome." Avoid "outcome" in new writing for this project. Apply this preference going forward. The production codebase still uses "outcome" as its umbrella term — see § Codebase outcome codes below for the one-to-one mapping to editorial sector codes.

Landscape (umbrella term)

  • COEQWAL Landscape — the conceptual frame. Described in geometric_framework.html.

The codebase predates the editorial term shift from "outcome" to "sector." It uses "outcome" as the umbrella term and suffixed identifiers (CWS_DEL, AG_REV, RES_STOR, etc.). Editorial writing uses "sector" and bare codes (CWS, AG, RES, WRC).

The codebase term is not being changed ahead of launch. Briefs that consume API data translate codebase identifiers to editorial labels at render time, using the display strings already defined in outcomes.ts as the source of truth. A codebase rename ("outcome" → "sector") is a deferred cleanup task, post-launch.

Theme brief naming

Level 1 theme briefs use sentence case in both inline prose and Related-briefs lists. The two canonical full forms most prone to inconsistency:

  • "Farms and groundwater" (not "Farms and Groundwater")
  • "Rivers, salmon and the Delta ecosystem" (not "Rivers and Salmon and the Delta Ecosystem", and not "Rivers and Salmon and Delta Ecosystem")

The other Level 1 names ("Community water systems", "The Delta as a living place") follow the same sentence-case rule.

BASELINES is the exception — display form is all caps, set in italics when referenced inline (BASELINES). This signals its role as the system-wide foundational brief, distinct from theme briefs.

Card-short forms. In tight horizontal display contexts like the Findings tab card grid, theme brief titles may use a shorter display form. The canonical card-short forms are:

  • "Farms and groundwater" (identical to the full form)
  • "Rivers and salmon" (short form for ECO; the full form "Rivers, salmon and the Delta ecosystem" is used inline and in Related-briefs lists)

The other two Level 1 themes fit the available card width without truncation and have no separate short form. Where present, the card teaser paragraph carries the longer framing that the short title doesn't.
