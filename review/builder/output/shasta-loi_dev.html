Reservoir storage

Modeled tier outcomes at Shasta Reservoir

1. Headline

At Shasta Reservoir, modeled tier values across the 72 COEQWAL scenarios range from Tier 2 to Tier 3. Of the 48 scenarios run under warmer-drier hydroclimates, 18 (38%) keep the same tier label their strategy has under today's climate and 30 (62%) shift to a different tier. Because the reported scenarios vary both the operating strategy and the hydroclimate, differences between scenarios combine climate and operations effects. These effects are addressed in section 4.

2. What this is

COEQWAL uses CalSim 3 — the state's main water-system model — to test how California's water system performs under different conditions. The model outputs are in the form of scenarios: each scenario is a pairing of a water management strategy (a way of operating the system, like today's rules or a proposed alternative) and a hydroclimate (patterns of temperature, rainfall, and snow that determine how much water is available over time). These results are sorted into four levels, called tiers: Optimal, Acceptable, At-risk, and Critical. Tiers describe what the model produces; they don't tell anyone what to do about it.

The model also tracks specific places in the study domain, which COEQWAL terms Locations of Interest, or LOIs. Cities, reservoirs, river reaches, farm regions, groundwater basins, and stretches of the Delta are each tracked individually. Every LOI sits inside one of nine area-level summaries COEQWAL uses to organize results (community water, agriculture, environmental flows, the Delta, salmon, in-Delta freshwater, Delta exports, reservoir storage, and groundwater).

This brief reports modeled tier values for one LOI — Shasta Reservoir (reservoir storage area) across 72 scenarios from the COEQWAL library. It consists of 24 water management strategies paired with 3 hydroclimates, and is the same set used for every LOI brief.

4. Operations and climate

Because every scenario pairs a strategy with a hydroclimate, a difference in tier value between two scenarios can come from two places: changes in the strategy used to run the system, or changes in the hydroclimate the system is run under. At an LOI like Shasta Reservoir, both axes are reported, so the difference between scenarios can be separated into a strategy part and a hydroclimate part.

Holding the hydroclimate fixed at today's climate, the spread among operating strategies at Shasta Reservoir is the strategy effect: 21 of the 24 strategies sit at Tier 2, while 3 sit at Tier 3. Holding the strategy fixed at current operations, the change across hydroclimates is the climate effect: the tier label sits at Tier 2 under today's climate and Tier 3 under Warmer and Drier I and Tier 3 under Warmer and Drier II. The remaining cells in the matrix combine both effects; the finer within-tier movement that the published brief will report requires the continuous tier values still in progress.

Figure 2 shows this decomposition for every scenario. Each dot is one modeled strategy under one hydroclimate; current operations (s0020) is gold. Within each column, strategies keep a fixed left-to-right order, so a strategy occupies the same relative position in every column and can be followed across the 3 hydroclimates.

Read against the BASELINES theme brief, the climate column at Shasta Reservoir under current operations is the climate-only reference for this place. Read against the Farms and Groundwater and Rivers and Salmon and Delta Ecosystem theme briefs, the rows under alternative strategies show how operating choices shift tier values here.

5. Pattern across scenarios

Looking across the full scenario matrix in Figure 1, modeled tier labels at Shasta Reservoir stay between Tier 2 and Tier 3. Within each hydroclimate column: under Today's climate, strategies span Tier 2 to Tier 3; under Warmer and Drier I, strategies span Tier 2 to Tier 3; under Warmer and Drier II, strategies span Tier 2 to Tier 3.

6. About this place

Shasta Reservoir sits on the upper Sacramento River north of Redding, behind Shasta Dam, and is operated by the Bureau of Reclamation as the cornerstone of the federal Central Valley Project (CVP). It is California's largest reservoir, with a total storage capacity of about 4,552 TAF. Its primary functions are water supply, flood control, hydropower, and cold-water releases that regulate temperatures in the upper Sacramento River for salmon downstream of the dam.

Variable End-of-month storage (TAF)

As the CVP's largest reservoir, Shasta captures runoff from the Sacramento, McCloud, and Pit river watersheds, and its end-of-month storage is the variable the model tracks here. The stored volume reflects the balance between inflow and releases for downstream deliveries, instream flows, temperature management, and flood-control space — so storage at this reservoir shapes water supply across the Sacramento Valley and conditions in the river below the dam, including the cold-water pool that winter-run Chinook salmon depend on.

8. What this doesn't say

This brief reports the tier results COEQWAL has generated based on what the CalSim 3 model produces under specific assumptions for one place. It doesn't predict what will happen at Shasta Reservoir, provide judgments about the modeled tier values, or recommend any course of action. The tier names (Optimal, Acceptable, At-risk, Critical) describe modeled conditions; what they mean for any particular community, decision, or person is not something the model itself decides.

The tier values shown reflect 72 scenarios from the COEQWAL library — pairings of 24 strategies with 3 hydroclimates — at this single LOI. They don't span every possible scenario, don't quantify uncertainty around the modeled values, and don't characterize other places. Other LOIs in the reservoir storage sector, in different parts of the system, may show different patterns; see the area summary in the theme briefs and the explorer for the full LOI set. This dev build additionally reports tier labels at whole-number resolution only: differences within a tier are real in the underlying model results but are not visible here.
