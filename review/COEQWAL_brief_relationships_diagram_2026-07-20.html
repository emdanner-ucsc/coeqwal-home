COEQWAL website “briefs”: justification and description

Added 2026-07-20 (Eric’s text). This prose pairs with the diagram above: the diagram shows the relationships; the text carries them for readers — and machine readers — who cannot see the arrows. A public rendering of this page must strip the internal status shown in the diagram.

Briefs are short (1–2 page), curated, printable summaries. These are static, citable, versioned outputs treated as scientific artifacts of the COEQWAL project. They are built by a Python pipeline that renders every value from the data API with a verification audit trail.

Their purpose is to fill the gap between the site’s front matter and the data explorer: without them, a visitor can’t get a quick summary of results without spending a long time driving the explorer themselves. The briefs serve three key purposes at once:

  1. Discoverability: the interactive tools are opaque to search engines and AI systems, so briefs are the only way COEQWAL’s content reaches search engines and AI systems with the framing intact.
  2. A bridge: each brief is a worked example teaching a cold-arrival reader how to interpret scenarios, hydroclimates, and tiers before they ever open the explorer.
  3. Setting the narrative: briefs are where the framing discipline meets readers with the right length to be effective, so readers don’t supply their own (potentially wrong) interpretive frame.

There are two classes of briefs:

  1. Results briefs: report modeled tier outcomes (tiers only; briefs do not report the in-depth data). These include the baselines brief, theme briefs, LOI briefs, sector briefs.
  2. Reference briefs: describe the components of the COEQWAL process without reporting results. These include strategy briefs, hydroclimate briefs, tier-calculation references.
