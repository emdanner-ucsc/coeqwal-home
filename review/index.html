Brief prototypes for review

Every brief prototype that is ready for the team to read, in one place. Each link opens the live prototype in a new tab. Below the list is a short note on the decision we still need from reviewers. The numbers in the Folsom and Baselines briefs are stand-ins, not real model results — see the notes on each.

What we need your input on

  1. What should the headline figure look like? Two candidate forms for the lead figure on a brief. One is a trade-off plot: two measures on two axes (for example, water for farms left-to-right and water for the environment bottom-to-top), so each scenario is a point and you can see what is traded against what. The other is a radial (wheel-shaped) plot, where each spoke is one of the nine outcome areas and the distance out the spoke shows how that area scores. This choice affects all four themes, and the figures stay as placeholders until it is settled.

How to give feedback. For general reactions, reply to the email this link came with, or leave a comment in the brief's Google Doc. To suggest specific wording, open the brief's Google Doc — linked under the three core briefs below — and edit it directly; turn on Suggesting mode so changes show as tracked suggestions. Several people can edit the same doc at once, so there is no file to send back.
