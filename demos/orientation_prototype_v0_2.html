How the library is organized

Every result in COEQWAL comes from one operational strategy paired with one hydroclimate. This page builds that picture up from a single cell to the whole library of 24 × 4 = 96 scenarios, then shows how to read any result in it.

Start from where we are today.

Every scenario in the library is one operational strategy paired with one hydroclimate. The clearest way in is to start from today and watch how results can move away from it.

A good place to start is where we are today. Current operations (s0020), run under the historical hydroclimate (BL), give one scenario — the baseline. It is a reference point, not a target: every other result in the library is read against it.

First, operations can be run differently. Operations are the choices managers can make. Holding the historical hydroclimate fixed, the baseline is one of 24 operational strategies — it is the one that changes nothing, and the other 23 run the system in different ways.

24 operational strategies · s0020 = current operations

Second, the same operations meet a different climate. The hydroclimate is the condition the system is handed — not something we choose. Holding operations at today’s, current operations are run under four hydroclimates: the historical record (BL) and three projected futures (HC1–HC3). These four are the baseline together with its climate-projected versions.

4 hydroclimates:
BL + HC1–HC3

Do this for every combination. Running all 24 operational strategies under all 4 hydroclimates fills out the whole library — 24 × 4 = 96 scenarios. The row above is the top edge of this grid; the column is its left edge; the two cross at the baseline in the top-left corner.

How to read any result.

The grid gives every result an address: one operational strategy, one hydroclimate. Reading a result means knowing which kind of cell you are in — and which cells to compare it against.

Every scenario sits on one of four pathways. Cross the two ingredients and the library splits into four quadrants. Pathway 1 is the baseline — current operations under the historical record. Pathway 2 changes only the operations. Pathway 3 keeps today’s operations but hands them a different climate. Pathway 4 changes both. The four counts add back up to the full library: 1 + 23 + 3 + 69 = 96.

Three results read any cell. Two grounding points anchor every comparison. The baseline says where we are now. Current operations under the hydroclimate you selected says how much change the climate brings on its own, before any management choice. Your scenario is then read against both: the step from anchor 1 to anchor 2 is the climate effect, and the step from anchor 2 to your scenario is the operations effect. Pick the historical record and anchors 1 and 2 coincide — there is no climate effect. Keep current operations and your scenario is anchor 2 — there is no operations effect.
